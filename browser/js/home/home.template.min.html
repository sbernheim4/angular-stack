Jacob's Philosophy

My reasons for running are straightforward: I have love for the Brandeis community, I recognize many of our university’s best qualities, and can see many areas where we fall short. Over the next year, I want to go to work with dynamic and achievable solutions to make us as good as we can be.

The tone I seek to set is bold for a Student Union, yet detailed and doable, and centers on areas of transparency, access, and community. I care about building a Union that is more open and sensitive to student needs than ever. Together, we will work to build a bright future for the Brandeis community.

The Issues

Access

Advocate for increased off-hour and weekend health services, especially at the Brandeis Counseling Center

Start the conversation about reducing the practice of free student labor on campus

Continue the fight for free and accessible Menstrual Products

Call upon the administration to include a laundry quota in financial aid

Advocate for students facing food insecurity

Transparency

Release the budget.

Modernize the Student Union's resources and communications.

Create “Essential Guides” for Student Union and Brandeis administration functions

Do a better job engaging, and at points disagreeing, with administration members

Amend A-Board and Treasury procedures to better work for clubs with unique needs

Continue to grow the bond between the Student Union and the Graduate Student Association

Comunity

Guarantee funding parity for clubs representing diverse and marginalized groups

Promote a Student Union that reflects Brandeis Students

Work for a greener campus

Continue our work towards enacting universal bystander intervention training
